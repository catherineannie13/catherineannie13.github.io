Hi, I'm Catherine, a fourth-year computer science major at Minerva University, a highly-selective global university with whom I travel all over the globe. This experience has given me invaluable skills, not only with programming and data science, but also open-mindedness and the ability to approach a problem from a diverse range of perspectives. During my undergraduate course, I am travelling to San Francisco, South Korea, Germany, Argentina, India, Taiwan and the UK to overcome cultural barriers and build resilience in unfamiliar environments.

As someone who only discovered the world of coding 3 years ago, I have become extremely driven to learn and adapt to this new and exciting world. Since then, I have decided to major in computer science, completed hackathons, and several online courses such as Datacamp's Data Science Track. I have skills in data analysis, modelling and machine learning in Python, R and SQL/BigQuery, web design in HTML, CSS, and Javascript, and several research internships under my belt.

In summer 2023 I completed my first data science internship at King. Here, I worked with their Central Insights team to develop a compartment model to predict unknown and future player data. I harnessed several skills during this time including querying (using Google's BigQuery), understanding how models represent simplifications of real-world scenarios, and Python programming skills. I absolutely loved this internship, particularly being able to see the real-life applications of my work. I can't wait to see where my Data Science journey takes me next!

I am currently a teaching assistant for a computer science course (Modelling Complex Systems) with key skills in curriculum design and problem-solving. My long-term career goals are to use technology to advance scientific research, whether this be at a company aligned with this aim, or at a my own startup. I also have strong interests in the applications of data science to both health and education.
